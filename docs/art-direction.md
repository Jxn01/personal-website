# JXN-000 · ART DIRECTION REFERENCES

Reference imagery sent by Norbert (inline, in chat). This file records each
reference and — more importantly — **what we're extracting from it**. Per
Norbert's instruction: *"don't hyperfocus on them, just pick out the best
parts and work it into a coherent design"* and *"don't make the whole website
about music."*

Standing rule: the JXN-000 spec's NO-list still governs. These references are
raw ore — we take **texture, palette tension, type energy, and humor
register**, never literal iconography (no boomboxes, graffiti fonts, artist
imagery on the site itself).

## BATCH 1 — received 2026-06-11

### B1-01 · "Boom Bap Beats" retro album cover (illustration)
Two producers at synths/mixers, 70s print style. Mustard yellow shirts, sky-cyan
background, warm browns, big speaker stacks. Visible print grain.
**Extract:**
- Halftone / offset-print texture as a surface treatment (sleeve cards, image
  treatments) — analog print artifact on a digital site.
- The palette *tension*: cold cyan-blue field × warm mustard/gold subjects.
  Strong signal for the accent decision (cold cyan + champagne gold).
- "Record sleeve as physical artifact" energy — paper, ink, edge wear (subtle).

### B1-02 · "Boombap" graffiti hand-style tag (black on white)
Single confident hand-lettered tag with halo and quote marks.
**Extract:**
- NOT the letterforms (spec bans graffiti fonts). What we take: the idea of
  **one single hand-made mark** existing on an otherwise machine-precise site —
  e.g. a hand-drawn signature/scribble in the liner-notes footer. One gesture,
  drawn once, never repeated. The human glitch in the system.

### B1-03 · Kendrick Lamar mixed-media poster
Mint/teal field, black stencil-cut display type top and bottom, portrait built
from collage: street-map texture filling the face, halftone dots, gold foil
scraps, scratch marks, denim texture.
**Extract:**
- **The portrait treatment.** Strongest single idea in the batch: a duotone
  portrait whose silhouette is filled/textured with *meaningful* material.
  For Norbert's photo: consider circuit-trace / city-grid / code texture
  resolving inside the silhouette (Budapest map texture is an option).
- Stencil-cut display energy for a few oversized words (hero scale) — sharp
  grotesque cut hard, not an actual stencil font.
- Controlled collage chaos: small scattered marks (×s, dots, dashes) as
  micro-texture — translates to our technical-label glyphs.
- Mint/teal × black × gold-foil: second strong vote for cyan-family accent
  with gold jewelry.

### B1-04 · B&W golden-era graffiti mural (illustration)
Dense monochrome wall: MC with mic, boombox, "BOOM BAP MAD FLOW / GOLDEN ERA /
BACK IN THE DAY" lettering everywhere.
**Extract:**
- One-color discipline at high density — a composition can be *loud* while
  using exactly one ink. Applies to our mono-label micro-copy layers.
- Background-wall energy: faint dense texture layers sitting far behind
  content (our dot grid / scanlines play this role).
- Nothing literal. No mics, no boomboxes, no slogan lettering.

### B1-05 · B&W photo: cat in bucket hat with chain, guy with boombox, NYC street
Documentary 90s-street photography style, heavy grain, deadpan.
**Extract:**
- **The humor register.** Completely straight-faced absurdity, zero winking.
  This is the site's voice in image form (the calculator that proves Euler's
  theorem lives in this register).
- B&W/duotone photographic grain treatment for photography.
- The chain = the gold accent philosophy: one glint of jewelry on an otherwise
  muted subject.

### Batch 1 — cross-cutting signals
1. **Palette:** repeated cyan/mint-family cold field × warm gold/mustard accents.
   Two of five references vote this way independently. (Final call still
   Norbert's, with batch 2 pending.)
2. **Texture:** halftone/print-grain everywhere — translate analog print
   artifacts into the techwear surface system, subtly.
3. **Type:** display type wants to be *cut*, heavy, confident — grotesque
   pushed to poster scale.
4. **The one human mark:** a single hand gesture (signature) against machine
   precision.
5. **Humor:** deadpan, never explained.

## BATCH 2 — received 2026-06-11

### B2-01 · The cat again (duplicate of B1-05)
Sent twice. Counted as a **double vote** for the deadpan B&W documentary
register. The cat is canon.

### B2-02 · Vaporwave pixel-art desktop (cyan field, pink/magenta windows)
Retro OS desktop: title-barred windows (`WEATHER.exe`, `rain.mp4`,
`ocean.mpeg`), taskbar with Start button and `4:20 AM` clock, pixel icons.
**Extract:**
- NOT the palette (pink/magenta/pastel violates the no-purple rule and the
  techwear base). What survives: **OS-chrome as a framing device** — title
  bars, window controls, a taskbar — for the site's overlay surfaces: the
  terminal window, `/status`, possibly project media framed as named windows
  (`jxn-001.mp4` energy).
- Strict two-color UI discipline inside a window: chrome in one ink, content
  in the palette. Maps cleanly onto our accent + ink system.
- The labeled-file naming joke (`plants.exe`) fits the site's deadpan register.

### B2-03 · neofetch terminal screenshot (ASCII Apple logo + system info)
Black terminal, colored ASCII art left, `key: value` system readout right,
terminal color-swatch strip at the bottom.
**Extract:**
- **Direct feature confirmation:** the site terminal gets a `fetch` command —
  neofetch-style readout with custom ASCII art (JXN mark or the cat — decide
  later) and real site/server stats: OS, uptime, stack, catalog number.
- The `key: value` mono readout IS our NATO-label metadata styling — same
  layout language for project card meta and the /status page.
- The terminal **color-swatch strip** as a tiny flex: the site's actual design
  tokens rendered as swatches in the footer or /status. Design system as
  liner-note credit.

### B2-04 · THE PORTRAIT (canonical) — Norbert, warm outdoor light
Curly hair, smirk, chain necklace, patterned shirt, soft warm background bokeh.
**Extract:**
- This is the spec's "warm portrait." Treatment per spec: slight
  duotone/desaturation to sit in the cold palette **without losing the smirk**.
- He is literally wearing the chain — the gold accent philosophy embodied.
  When duotoning, the chain glint is the one element allowed to stay warm
  (gold pass), if technically clean. Jewelry on the techwear, in the image
  itself.
- Pairs with the B1-03 treatment: silhouette resolving from dither/texture
  into the duotone portrait on scroll.
- ⚠ Need the original file delivered into the repo (chat images arrive
  inline). Placeholder box until then.

### B2-05 · "To Pimp a Butterfly" album cover
B&W group photo mounted inside a plain warm-grey sleeve border, advisory badge
bottom-right.
**Extract:**
- **The sleeve-mount framing:** photography presented as an artifact — image
  block inset within a flat border field, generous margins, nothing bleeding.
  Use for the portrait and project imagery: mounted, not full-bleed.
- Monochrome documentary grade with deep blacks — consistent with B1-05.
- The advisory badge is explicitly banned by the spec and stays banned. The
  *idea* that survives: a small rectangular mono **metadata stamp** (catalog
  number, side, year) placed with that same corner discipline.

### Batch 2 — cross-cutting signals
1. **Terminal is load-bearing:** two of five references are screen/OS culture.
   The terminal + OS-chrome overlays graduate from easter egg to a core
   design surface.
2. **Cyan survives another round** (vaporwave field), pink/purple explicitly
   does not. Cold cyan × champagne gold remains the leading palette candidate.
3. **Mounted, not full-bleed:** imagery as sleeve-framed artifact.
4. **The portrait is canon:** warm, smirking, chain on. Duotone to fit, glint
   stays.
